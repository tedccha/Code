# Meeting Brief Template

Use this exact structure when composing briefs. Keep each section tight — 3–5 bullets max.
Strip any section that has no meaningful data rather than writing "N/A".

---

## Single-Attendee Meeting

```
🗓 Prep: [Meeting Title] — [Day, Date @ Time]

👤 [Full Name] — [Title] at [Company]

**Who They Are**
- [Current role + tenure if known]
- [Relevant background: prior company/role if notable]
- [How you know them / relationship context from CRM]
- 🔗 [Dex profile](https://getdex.com/appv3/contacts/[id]) *(omit if id unavailable)*

**Last Interaction**
- [Date] — [What was discussed / outcome] *(source: Dex / Gmail thread / Tana notes)*
- [If Gmail thread fetched: key points, commitments, tone]
- [If Tana notes found and more recent: surface that content here]
- Open items: [any pending reminders or action items from Dex]

**LinkedIn Activity** (last 30 days)
- Posted about: [topic 1], [topic 2]
- [Any notable career update, promotion, job change]

**Company News**
- [Recent funding / product launch / partnership / press]
- [Any headwinds: layoffs, leadership changes, controversy]

**Suggested Talking Points**
- [Based on their recent posts — show you've paid attention]
- [Follow-up from last interaction]
- [Relevant to meeting topic]

**Quick Context**
[1–2 sentence bottom line: the most important thing to know walking in]
```

---

## Multi-Attendee Meeting

Add a "Meeting Context" block at the top, then one section per attendee:

```
🗓 Prep: [Meeting Title] — [Day, Date @ Time]

**Meeting Context**
- Purpose: [what this meeting is about]
- Attendees: [Name (Role)], [Name (Role)], ...
- Dynamic: [e.g., "Sarah is the decision-maker; Tom is technical evaluator"]

---

👤 [Attendee 1 Name] — [Title] at [Company]
[... same structure as single-attendee above ...]

---

👤 [Attendee 2 Name] — [Title] at [Company]
[... same structure ...]
```

---

## Formatting Notes

- Use `**bold**` for section headers (renders in Tana)
- Keep bullet text under 15 words each
- Dates: "March 14" not "2026-03-14"
- If a whole section is empty (no data found), omit it entirely
- The "Quick Context" line is mandatory — always write it, even if research is thin

---

## Large Meeting Footer (>5 attendees)

Add this at the bottom of any brief where attendees were capped:

```
👥 Additional attendees not prepped: [Name], [Name], [Name]...
   To prep any of them, say: "Prep [Name] from [meeting title]"
```
